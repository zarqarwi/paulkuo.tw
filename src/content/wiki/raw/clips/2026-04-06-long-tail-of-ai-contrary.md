While all eyes are on AI-focused companies, the number of "non-AI" companies that will be impacted by artificial intelligence far outnumbers their AI-focused counterparts, collectively referred to as "the long tail of AI."

This Contrary Research deep dive examines the broader economic impact of AI beyond the headline-grabbing AI-native startups, arguing that the real transformation will happen in the vast majority of traditional businesses that adopt AI tools and workflows.

The long-tail perspective suggests that the biggest economic value from AI won't come from a few dominant AI companies, but from the massive number of traditional businesses that integrate AI into their operations — a distribution that follows heavy-tail economics.
