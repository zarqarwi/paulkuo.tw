The rise of LLMs has transformed software engineering, with agents emerging as the primary building blocks in the multi-LLM era of 2026. This shift challenges traditional microservices architectures by introducing dynamic, autonomous components capable of complex decision-making and collaboration.

In practice, production teams typically use two models: a smaller, faster model handles routine tool calls and data retrieval, while a larger reasoning model handles planning and error recovery. The cost difference between these two tiers can be 10–20x.

The article argues that agents are becoming the new unit of composition in software systems, replacing the microservice as the fundamental building block. This has implications for how we think about system design, deployment, and observability.

Key themes: multi-agent orchestration, two-tier model routing (fast + reasoning), cost optimization, the shift from static microservices to dynamic agent-based architectures.
