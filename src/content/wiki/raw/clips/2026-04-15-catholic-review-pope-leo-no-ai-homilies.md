Pope Leo XIV has told priests directly: do not use AI to prepare homilies, and do not chase social media engagement metrics ("likes on TikTok").

His theological argument: AI "will never be able to share faith." A homily is an act of pastoral discernment and incarnational witness — not a content-generation task. Outsourcing it to AI hollows out the ministry itself.

The paired warning against TikTok likes frames the issue as broader than just homily-writing: it's about clergy avoiding the attention economy's logic entirely.
