Taiwan has revised its plastics reduction policy, scaling back reliance on outright bans and setting new targets that place greater emphasis on economic incentives and circular economy measures. Ministry of Environment will aim to:

- Cut single-use petrochemical plastics products by 5% by 2030
- 10% by 2035
- 2024 as the baseline year

Policy shift reflects a mature circular economy approach: moving from command-and-control prohibition to market-based circularity incentives. Connects to broader 2050 Circular Economy Roadmap scheduled for finalization in 2026.

Complementary context: Taiwan's circular economy sector growing at ~9% annually (vs Germany's 2.3%). Green technology sector valued at NT$512.3 billion ($16.3B), with ~30% (NT$168.8B) from circular economy activities.
