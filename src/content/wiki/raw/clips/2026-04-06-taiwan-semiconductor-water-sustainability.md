By 2026, TSMC's reclaimed water facility is expected to support up to 36,000 tonnes of reclaimed water daily, enough to meet 60% of the company's local demand by 2030. However, this leaves a 40% reliance on traditional reservoirs — a vulnerable reliance as Taiwan faces longer dry seasons and fewer typhoons.

With a carbon fee trial set for 2025 and full rollout in 2026, Taiwan is aligning with global sustainability policies while strengthening its long-term competitiveness. Additionally, official implementation of CBAM (EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism) will be effective on January 1, 2026.

TSMC set a target to reach peak carbon emissions this year, reduce them to 2020 levels by 2030, and to use renewable energy for 60% of global operations by 2040.

Taiwan needs to acknowledge, as a whole, the interconnection of economic security, environmental security, semiconductor security, and energy security.
