The US Department of Labor released an AI literacy framework on February 13, 2026. This is a significant policy development as the first major government-level AI skills framework from a labor department.

Key context from related search results:

Three forces converge in 2026 that make reskilling assessments central to business strategy: (1) Automation no longer replaces entire professions — it replaces parts of them. (2) The most valuable organizational tool is no longer a learning management system or a training catalog — it's the reskilling assessment. (3) 2026 marks the shift from training programs to skill intelligence, from teaching employees to measuring adaptability.

Enterprise AI capability falls into four distinct layers, and not all skills carry equal weight. The most important AI and data skills in 2026 are not deeply technical, but interpretive, applied, and judgment-driven.

A framework for getting real, compounding value out of AI is structured as five proficiency levels, emphasizing that AI proficiency is not about knowing tools but about building judgment.
