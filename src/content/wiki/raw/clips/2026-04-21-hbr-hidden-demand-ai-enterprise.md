## Main Argument

The article highlights a critical disconnect in corporate AI adoption. While official enterprise AI programs often struggle with "clunky tools, slow rollouts, and unimpressive results," employees are simultaneously adopting consumer AI tools independently.

## Key Finding: The Hidden Revolution

The authors describe what they call a "hidden revolution" occurring within large organizations. Employees bypass institutional limitations by using personal versions of ChatGPT, Claude, and similar models on their own devices, frequently without organizational knowledge or oversight.

## Concrete Example

The authors cite a telling case: "when their employees are working on their secure, no-AI, bank-issued PCs, they often have their personal laptops open" to consumer AI platforms. This observation comes from an official at a major central bank.

## Core Issues

- Corporate gen AI initiatives frequently deliver disappointing outcomes
- Employees lack adequate access to approved AI tools
- Cumbersome internal systems drive workaround adoption
- Compliance and IT departments remain unaware of widespread shadow AI use

## Authorship

The piece is authored by researchers from BBVA, London School of Economics, and various Spanish universities with expertise in AI adoption, economics, and organizational knowledge management.
