New Lines Institute analysis of Taiwan's semiconductor sustainability challenges and their global strategic implications.

Key findings:
- Taiwan's carbon fee trial set for 2025, full rollout in 2026, aligning with global sustainability policies
- TSMC STSP Reclaimed Water Plant expected to support 36,000 tonnes of reclaimed water daily by 2026, meeting 60% of local demand by 2030 — but still 40% reliance on traditional reservoirs
- TSIA issued rare public warning about critical power shortages threatening chip manufacturing dominance
- Taiwanese manufacturers lag behind international competitors in RE100 progress; US, Japan, South Korea facilities already meeting renewable energy targets
- SEMICON Taiwan Green Manufacturing Area expanded 30%, centered on "Circular Economy Insights"
- Taiwan needs to acknowledge the interconnection of economic security, environmental security, semiconductor security, and energy security

The article frames sustainability not just as environmental concern but as national security and economic competitiveness issue.
