America Magazine's coverage of Pope Leo XIV's 60th World Day of Social Communications message. Key teachings:

**Core warning**: "The stakes are high. The power of simulation is such that AI can even deceive us by fabricating parallel 'realities,' usurping our faces and voices. We are immersed in a world of multidimensionality where it is becoming increasingly difficult to distinguish reality from fiction."

**Pope's stance on digital innovation**: "The task laid before us is not to stop digital innovation, but rather to guide it and to be aware of its ambivalent nature. It is up to each of us to raise our voice in defence of human persons, so that we can truly assimilate these tools as allies."

**Content labeling call**: "Content generated or manipulated by AI must be clearly labeled and distinguished from content created by humans." Authorship, ownership and copyright must be protected.

**Collective responsibility**: All stakeholders — tech industry, legislators, creative companies, academia, artists, journalists, educators — must be involved in building informed and responsible digital citizenship.

World Day of Social Communications 2026 celebrated May 17, 2026.
