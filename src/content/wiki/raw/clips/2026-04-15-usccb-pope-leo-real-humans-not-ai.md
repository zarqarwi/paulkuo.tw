USCCB (US Conference of Catholic Bishops) reports Pope Leo XIV's position: humanity must not allow AI to "obscure, exploit or suppress human voices, needs, knowledge, talents, creativity and critical thinking abilities."

The Pope's directive: "Content generated or manipulated by AI must be clearly labeled and distinguished from content created by humans."

This is framed as a media-ethics and communications-policy stance, distinct from the Pope's earlier homiletic guidance. The USCCB coverage positions it as Catholic doctrine for the AI era — not a ban on AI, but a labeling and transparency mandate.
