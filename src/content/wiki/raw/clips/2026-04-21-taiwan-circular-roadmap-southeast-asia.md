## Key Initiative

Taiwan unveiled a draft 2050 Circular Economy Roadmap at the Asia Pacific Circular Economy Roundtable in October 2025, aiming to become "the hub of the circular economy in Asia." The government targets:
- Doubling resource productivity
- Reducing per-capita material consumption by **30%**
- Increasing circularity rates to **2.5 times 2020 levels**

## Strategic Goals

The roadmap emphasizes reforms in eco-design, circular procurement, and innovation to shift from waste management toward complete resource circulation. Taiwan's limited natural resources and export-dependent industries make this transition critical for supply chain resilience in semiconductors, petrochemicals, and machinery sectors.

## Regional Cooperation

Officials stress collaboration is essential. "Circular economy is highly localized yet not something that can be done by one country alone," the environment minister noted. Taiwan leverages its New Southbound Policy to deepen ties with Southeast Asian partners through trade, investment, and technology sharing.

## Southeast Asian Readiness

Progress varies regionally. Vietnam adopted circular policies influenced by Taiwan's approach, while the Philippines implemented an Extended Producer Responsibility Act in 2022. However, enforcement remains weak, and replicating Taiwan's initiatives faces challenges due to local infrastructure differences.

## Implementation Philosophy

Taiwan officials emphasize cooperation over compliance, contrasting their approach with EU regulatory models. The strategy involves establishing internal systems first, then adapting experience to partner nations' specific contexts rather than imposing uniform standards.
