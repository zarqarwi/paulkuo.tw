Taiwan is accelerating circular economy efforts through new legislation, green funding, and AI-driven industrial upgrades. President Lai announced the 2050 circular economy roadmap will be unveiled in 2026.

Key targets: double resource productivity, cut per-capita material use by ~30%, raise circularity rate to 2.5x of 2020 level. Taiwan's circular economy sector growing at ~9% annually (vs Germany's ~2.3%).

New voluntary "circular label" scheme launched March 2026, covering plastics and textiles. Environment ministry funding industry-academia collaboration for recycling technologies (e.g. converting discarded textiles into construction materials).
