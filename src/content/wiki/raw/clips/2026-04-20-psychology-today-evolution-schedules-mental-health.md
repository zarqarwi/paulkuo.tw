Psychology Today's January 2026 analysis of modern work patterns through evolutionary psychology lens:

**Core insight**: In modern organizations, what was once lifesaving in rare bursts has become a constant hum, turning our days into a string of micro-emergencies where fears about the future bleed into present reactions.

**Mindset shift**: The organizational mindset has shifted from "getting ahead" to "holding on", from "chasing opportunity" to "protecting stability", and from a growth mindset to survival mode.

**Stress statistics (2026)**:
- Roughly 40% of employees worldwide said they experienced a lot of stress during the previous day (Gallup State of the Global Workplace 2026)
- Above pre-pandemic levels for several years
- Nearly 46% of technology professionals reported feeling burned out in 2026, compared to 31% in 2024
- 66% of US employees today report feeling burnout in some form

**AI-efficiency paradox**: Many professionals now face a strange equation: the responsibilities of several roles, supported by AI tools, but still carried by a single human operator. Rather than reducing workload, the work did not disappear after the layoffs and was redistributed among the remaining team members.

**Breaking the cycle**: Quiet thriving — small, intentional shifts that give work back some meaning. Core concept is agency — recognition that we are reacting to demands but also choosing how to respond.
