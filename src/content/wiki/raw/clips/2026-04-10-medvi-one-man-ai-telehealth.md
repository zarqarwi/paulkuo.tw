Matthew Gallagher launched Medvi, a GLP-1 telehealth startup, from his Los Angeles home in September 2024 with $20,000, no employees, and over a dozen AI tools. Key metrics:
- $401 million in sales in first full year
- 250,000 customers
- 16.2% net profit margin
- Tracking toward $1.8 billion in 2026 revenue

AI tools used: ChatGPT, Claude and Grok for code and copy; Midjourney and Runway for ad creative; ElevenLabs for voice-based customer communication.

Important caveats: The FDA had already warned the company prior to media coverage. The site allegedly featured deepfaked before-and-after patient photos and other concerning practices. This case study illustrates both the extraordinary potential and the ethical risks of AI-powered solo entrepreneurship.
