## Study Overview

Peer-reviewed research (SusMT, 2026) examining the technical and economic viability of a modified recycling process for waste printed circuit boards (WPCBs), analyzing both environmental benefits and financial returns from precious metal recovery.

## Key Technical Findings

Evaluated a 500-ton-per-year processing facility. Base metals demonstrated excellent recovery rates, while precious metals showed meaningful yields. **Gold recovery approaching 80%** represents the most significant precious metal achievement, making it the primary economic driver.

## Economic Analysis Results

- Baseline scenario: **Net Present Value (NPV) = 46.7 million euros**
- Sensitivity range: 20.2 to 50.9 million euros depending on gold pricing
- Break-even point: 4,148 euros per kilogram of gold
- Even under adverse market scenarios, the investment maintains positive returns

## Critical Success Factors

Gold price is the most influential variable affecting profitability, followed by metal purity and WPCB acquisition costs.

## Circular Economy Impact

Beyond financial metrics, WPCB recycling advances sustainability objectives:
- Reduces electronic waste streams
- Recovers strategic materials essential for resource security
- Supports UN Sustainable Development Goal 12

## Context

Waste printed circuit boards account for approximately 3–6% by weight of total e-waste and typically contain around 30% metals. PCBs are the only category of e-waste for which recycling is consistently profitable irrespective of the technology employed.
