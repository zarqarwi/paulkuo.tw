Taiwan will launch a new voluntary "circular label" scheme to encourage companies to adopt resource-efficient production and recycling practices, initially covering four product categories including plastics and textiles.

Taiwan's circular economy sector is growing at about 9% annually, compared with roughly 2.3% in Germany. Taiwan's green technology sector is currently valued at NT$512.3 billion (US$16.3 billion), of which about 30% comes from circular economy activities.

The government plans to promote circular economy development across four areas: institutional coordination, legislation, technology deployment and financing. A circular economy advisory council comprising industry and academia representatives will focus on critical metals, biological resources and industrial material recycling. The ministry is partnering with Taiwan AI School to expand industrial AI capabilities, and deploying a NT$10 billion (US$318 million) green growth fund to support corporate transition.

Long-term targets include doubling resource productivity, cutting per-capita material use by about 30%, and raising the circularity rate to 2.5 times the 2020 level.
