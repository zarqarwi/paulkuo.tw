ResearchGate discussion framing career choice explicitly as a fat-tail (heavy-tail) decision rather than a bell-curve one.

The practical consequence: when variance in outcomes is huge, the optimal strategy shifts from "pick the path with the highest average expected return" to "maximize exploration early so you can move toward the tail." 80,000 Hours' career-advice framework is cited as a concrete implementation — exploration as an information-gathering investment, not a hedge.

Two operational implications:
1. **More trials, not better averages** — you need to try many options to increase the chance of hitting a tail outcome.
2. **A compass, not a GPS** — you need coarse directional heuristics that push you toward the tail, because you cannot forecast tail positions precisely.
