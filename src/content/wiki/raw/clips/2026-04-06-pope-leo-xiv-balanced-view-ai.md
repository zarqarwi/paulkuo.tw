Pope Leo XIV released his Message for the 60th World Day of Social Communications on March 6, 2026, entitled "Preserving Human Voices and Faces".

The Pope states that the "task laid before us is not to stop digital innovation, but rather to guide it and to be aware of its ambivalent nature", its obvious advantages and its less obvious risks. He emphasizes that "embracing the opportunities offered by digital technology and artificial intelligence with courage, determination and discernment does not mean turning a blind eye to critical issues, complexities and risks".

Pope Leo notes that artificial intelligence systems have increasingly taken control of the production of texts, music and videos, putting much of the human creative industry at risk of being dismantled and replaced with the label "Powered by AI".

The Pope wrote that "content generated or manipulated by AI must be clearly labeled and distinguished from content created by humans".

Pope Leo XIV invokes a triad of values to guide AI development: responsibility, cooperation and education—encompassing the responsibility of AI owners, creators and programmers, cooperation among various sectors, and education of users.

Pope Leo XIV told priests not to use artificial intelligence in preparing homilies, stressing that AI "will never be able to share faith".
