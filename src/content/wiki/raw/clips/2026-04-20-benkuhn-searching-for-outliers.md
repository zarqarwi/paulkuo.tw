Ben Kuhn's foundational essay on heavy-tailed distributions in life decisions. Core thesis:

**Definition**: A heavy-tailed distribution is one where the top few percent of outcomes are a large multiple of the typical or median outcome.

**Key insight**: Lots of important things in life are outlier-driven — jobs, employees, relationships. Heavy-tailed distributions are really unintuitive to most people, since all the "action" happens in the tiny fraction of samples that are outliers.

**Career application**: Practical example is location choice. If you were a software engineer, moving to San Francisco would have been likely to put you on a very different career trajectory than almost any other city because there are so many more jobs available, and different cities are outliers for different people.

**Strategic implication**: If you're aware of when you're working on something that involves sampling from a heavy-tailed distribution, you can avoid those mistakes and end up with much better outcomes. Recognizing when career outcomes follow heavy-tailed patterns — where a few exceptional results dominate — can help make better strategic decisions about where to focus.

**Practical advice**: Increase variance intentionally, broaden your search funnel, don't settle on early-looking "good enough" samples when distribution is heavy-tailed.
