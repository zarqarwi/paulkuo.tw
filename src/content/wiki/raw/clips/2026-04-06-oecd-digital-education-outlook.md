OECD's 2026 report on generative AI in education. Key findings:

1. When embedded in sound teaching strategies, GenAI can strengthen argumentation skills and deepen subject knowledge in collaborative learning scenarios.
2. Without pedagogical guidance, outsourcing tasks to GenAI enhances performance with no real learning gains.
3. Offloading cognitive tasks to chatbots creates risks of "metacognitive laziness" that may deter skill acquisition long-term.
4. E-learning sector projecting growth exceeding $365B for 2026 (14% annual growth).
5. McKinsey projects 60% of global workforce will need new skills by 2030.
