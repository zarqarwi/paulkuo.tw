By leveraging machine learning, data analytics, and automation, AI technologies are transforming carbon accounting through predictive analytics for emissions forecasting, real-time monitoring systems, and automated data collection mechanisms.

AI platforms automatically match activity data to the most relevant emission factors across global databases and learn from specific business context over time, delivering up to 75x improvement in footprint accuracy compared to traditional methods.

Leading solutions include CO2 AI (covering Scope 1-3 emissions with AI-powered emission factor matching), Greenly (combining carbon footprint measurement, product lifecycle analysis, and ESG reporting), and Normative (science-based accounting engine specializing in Scope 3 emissions).

In January 2026, the EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) started its definitive period, with measurements throughout the year so certificate sales can begin in 2027. AI is now firmly on the ESG agenda, with attention expanding from AI's environmental footprint to examining supply chains and human rights.
