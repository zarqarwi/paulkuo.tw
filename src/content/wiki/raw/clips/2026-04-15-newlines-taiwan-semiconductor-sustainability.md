New Lines Institute argues Taiwan's semiconductor industry sits at the intersection of four interlocking security dimensions: economic security, environmental security, semiconductor security, and energy security.

Key data points from the analysis:
- Taiwan's carbon fee trial launched 2025, full rollout 2026
- TSMC's Southern Taiwan Science Park Reclaimed Water Plant: 36,000 tonnes/day by 2026, covering 60% of local demand by 2030
- TSMC net-zero target: 2050
- TSIA (Taiwan Semiconductor Industry Association) has issued a public warning on critical power shortages threatening Taiwan's chip dominance

The analytical move is to frame Taiwan's sustainability transition not as an ESG compliance exercise but as a **supply-chain security question** — if Taiwan cannot secure green energy at scale, the global chip supply chain is at geopolitical risk.
