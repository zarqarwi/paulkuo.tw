Austin Erkl's analysis of why achieving basic success creates a psychological trap — what he calls "survival mode success without purpose."

Key arguments:
- Your brain evolved for survival, not happiness. Uncertainty = danger, familiarity = safety (even when harmful)
- Once basic goals are met (food, shelter, security), the brain's "lizard mode" is satisfied — but this leads to stagnation, not fulfillment
- This is the comfort trap: survival mode success without purpose. It's not laziness.
- The longer you avoid discomfort, the more anxious you become. Neuroplasticity declines, self-belief erodes.
- Unused ambition turns into inner tension and resentment

Breaking free requires:
1. Make the status quo visible — document current patterns
2. Start small — train the brain that change is survivable
3. Shift from "survival why" (paycheck) to "purpose why" (legacy impact)
4. Purpose + community = breakout

The article draws on neuroscience research showing that comfort zones literally shrink brain adaptability over time.
