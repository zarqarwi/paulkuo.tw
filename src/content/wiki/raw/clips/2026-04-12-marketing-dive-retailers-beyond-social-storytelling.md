Retailers are exploring channels like Reddit and Substack as part of a "human-powered brand building" strategy, getting regular customers and creators to advocate on behalf of the brand. Brands need to build a narrative that AI can't flatten and humans won't forget.

Consistent brand storytelling can deliver up to 30% stronger long-term ROI than purely performance-led approaches. Brand storytelling in 2026 demands authenticity, immersive tech, and multi-platform narratives that evolve with audiences and deliver measurable impact.

Frameworks such as Pulp Strategy's Narrative Architecture integrate storytelling, data, and analytics dashboards to tie engagement, recall, and sentiment directly to ROI and lifetime value metrics. In a crowded, fast-moving market, the brands that win will be those that tell the clearest stories—and then deliver on them.
