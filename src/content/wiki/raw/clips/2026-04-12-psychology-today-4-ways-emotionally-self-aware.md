Emotional self-awareness is the ability to understand one's own emotions, thoughts, and values and how they influence behavior across contexts. It serves as the cornerstone of emotional intelligence.

Self-awareness requires that one's perception of self match reality, which means being reflective and open to feedback to develop a true sense of self. A realistic understanding allows better behavior management.

Key development strategies include: mindfulness practices (purposely focusing attention on the present moment), reflection and introspection, seeking feedback, and intentional self-growth. By understanding strengths and weaknesses, one can build on what makes them thrive while improving areas that hold them back.

Research reveals that students with social and emotional skills perform better academically, have stronger relationships, experience greater well-being, and engage in less risky behavior, with SEL skills positively impacting education, employment, and mental health outcomes into adulthood.
