ARC Magazine analysis of Pope Leo XIV's approach to AI as a defining theme of his papacy. The Pope has drawn explicit parallels between the current AI revolution and the Industrial Revolution that Pope Leo XIII faced, choosing his papal name to signal continuity with that legacy.

Key positions from Pope Leo XIV on AI in 2026:
- AI will not destroy civilization, but neither does it inherently improve lives
- Invokes a triad of values: responsibility, cooperation, and education
- Told priests not to use AI for homilies, stating AI "will never be able to share faith"
- Called for mandatory labeling of AI-generated content
- Warned AI puts the human creative industry at risk of being dismantled
- Hosted a Vatican AI conference with ~50 representatives from academia and tech
- On X: "every design choice expresses a vision of humanity"

The article notes the Vatican's unique position: "the only state or country that doesn't have a special interest," making the Pope's voice on AI ethics particularly significant.

The Pope frames AI governance through Catholic social teaching: "the Church offers to everyone the treasury of her social teaching in response to another industrial revolution."
