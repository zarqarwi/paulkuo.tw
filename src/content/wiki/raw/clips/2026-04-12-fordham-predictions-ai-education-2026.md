In 2026, the most successful AI deployments in education begin with teachers, not students. When AI reduces planning time, improves instructional clarity, and supports differentiation, teachers become advocates rather than resistors.

The OECD Digital Education Outlook 2026 suggests GenAI can support learning when guided by clear teaching principles, but outsourcing tasks to GenAI without pedagogical guidance simply enhances performance with no real learning gains.

The market size of AI in education is projected at $12.3B globally by 2026, growing 36% CAGR since 2022. On average 86% of students in schools and higher education utilize AI. Adaptive learning systems show 42% improvement in learning outcomes and 83% of institutions plan AI teaching assistant deployment by end of 2026.

However, 70% of teachers worry AI weakens critical thinking and research skills, and over half of students agree that using AI in class makes them feel less connected to their teachers.
