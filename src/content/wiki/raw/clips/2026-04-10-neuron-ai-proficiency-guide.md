The Neuron's AI proficiency framework for 2026, structured as five levels of AI competency.

Core insight: The gap between casual AI users and power users comes down to one thing — structure. Power users don't use fancier prompts; they build systems.

The framework emphasizes that real, compounding value from AI comes not from individual interactions but from systematic integration into workflows. This aligns with the broader 2026 trend of moving from "AI as tool" to "AI as infrastructure."

Additional context from the broader skill development landscape:
- IBM committed to skill 2 million people globally in AI by end of 2026
- WEF reports leading tech companies supporting 120+ million workers by 2030
- Boston Institute: 2026 marks the shift from training programs to skill intelligence
- AI upskilling through mentoring emerging as key delivery mechanism
