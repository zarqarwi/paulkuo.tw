## Revering the Unknown Tide
### Market Revelations Ten Years After the Financial Crisis

Ten years have passed since the financial crisis.
Taiwan's stock index has reached a five-year high.

Investors who followed the bearish market sentiment and chose to short the market
have already been carried out countless times.

This is not an isolated market phenomenon.
The U.S. stock market across the Pacific and South Korea's market to the north are similarly at relative five-year highs.

---

### The Dual Fuel of Capital and Outlook

What is the momentum driving these markets?
It is future outlook and capital with nowhere else to go.

Outlook is the spark that ignites markets.
Capital is the inexhaustible fuel.

Global money printing runs rampant.
Excess capital urgently seeks outlets for itself.
This sometimes leaves us wondering whether the situation is genuinely promising, or simply awash with speculative funds.

---

### Humbly Facing Market Upheavals

Markets change too rapidly.
We possess too much knowledge, but too little genuine understanding.

Rise and fall have nothing to do with which political party governs.
It is a torrent that transcends geography and politics.

Facing an unfathomable future.
Facing the intricate complexities of global capital dynamics.

We can hardly help but be humble.
