When AI can write cleaner, more efficient code than I can, what value can I still provide?

The concept of the "Post-Code Era" offers me inspiration:
Our true value is shifting from "executing technology" to "defining problems and cultivating taste."

## Cognitive Elevation Under Technological Democratization

The rise of generative programming platforms (like Cursor) has pushed syntax and tools to the background.

Truly valuable capability is no longer about writing lines of code.
It's about being able to define problems, decompose tasks, and clearly communicate requirements to AI in natural language.

What we call "Vibe coding" is this: when I can use minimal language to have AI implement functionality, I'm not "controlling machines" but "guiding logic."

## The Rise of Humanistic Literacy and Taste

In the AI era, linguistic sensitivity determines whether you can become a good designer.

Humanistic training (rhetoric, contextual interpretation) silently supports the quality of our interactions with AI.
"Taste" isn't vague preference, but the capacity to perceive "how things should be done" and "how things are worth doing."

I'm like a coach.
The AI model is running on the field, while I'm responsible for deciding tactics and value trade-offs.
AI can offer suggestions, but cannot define aesthetics, cultural depth, and social context—this is precisely the scarcity of human taste.

## From Code Craftsman to Value Curator

Future core competitiveness lies in "thinking accurately," not just "coding fast."

We're no longer merely satisfying user needs, but philosophically questioning: "What features are worth creating?"
The core of innovation has never been efficiency, but directional sense.

## Conclusion: Questions AI Cannot Proactively Answer

Humans won't be replaced by AI, but redefined.
We will increasingly become "curators of value."

Code can be copied, models can be trained.
But "what you choose to build"—this question AI can never proactively answer.
