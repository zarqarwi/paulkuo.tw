When AI can write cleaner, more efficient code than I can, what value can I still provide?

The concept of the "Post-Code Era" offers me inspiration:
Our true value is shifting from "executing technology" to "defining problems and shaping taste."

## Cognitive Elevation Under Technical Democratization

The rise of generative programming platforms (like Cursor) relegates syntax and tools to the background.

The truly valuable capability is no longer writing countless lines of code.
Rather, it's the ability to define problems, decompose tasks, and clearly communicate requirements to AI in natural language.

What we call "Vibe coding" means that when I can use minimal phrases to have AI implement functionality, I'm not "controlling the machine" but "guiding logic."

## The Rise of Humanities and Taste

In the AI era, linguistic sensitivity determines whether you can become a good designer.

Humanities training (rhetoric, contextual interpretation) silently supports the quality of our interactions with AI.
"Taste" is not vague preference, but the capacity to perceive "how things should be done" and "how things are worth doing."

I'm like a coach.
The AI model runs on the field, while I decide tactics and value trade-offs.
AI can offer suggestions, yet cannot define aesthetics, cultural depth, and social context—this is precisely the scarcity of human taste.

## From Code Craftsman to Value Curator

Future core competitiveness lies in "thinking accurately," not just "writing fast."

We no longer merely satisfy user needs, but pursue with philosophical depth: "What features are worth creating?"
The core of innovation has never been efficiency, but a sense of direction.

## Conclusion: Questions AI Cannot Proactively Answer

Humans won't be replaced by AI, but redefined.
We will increasingly resemble "value curators."

Code can be copied, models can be trained.
But "what you choose to build"—this question AI can never proactively answer.
