If you were a student today, what should you learn?
Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang gave a simple yet profound answer: "Learn AI."

This is not just career advice, but a structural response to our entire era.
We stand at the threshold of intelligent civilization, and must redefine "what is learning" and "where does value come from."

## First Layer: AI as a Mirror and Whetstone for Thinking

Jensen Huang says: "AI is like a smart child."

AI doesn't guess—it only responds to clear logic.
The prerequisite for effective AI collaboration isn't programming languages, but "linguistic logic."

You must transform vague intentions into concrete instructions.
Rather than saying we use AI, it's more accurate to say AI forces us to see our own blind spots in thinking. It is a mirror, reflecting the sharp edges of your language.

## Second Layer: AI Collaboration as Workplace Entry Ticket

Whatever you study, you should ask: "Can AI help me do this better?"

In the future workplace, success won't be about who works harder.
It will be about who better understands how to mobilize resources and amplify leverage.

Competition will no longer center on "what you know," but "what intelligence you can summon to walk alongside you."
AI collaboration isn't an extra bonus point, but basic literacy and an entry threshold—like typing.

## Third Layer: The Essential Shift in Education

When AI can rapidly summarize data and provide answers, what is the function of schools?

Future education must shift from "teaching knowledge" to "teaching people how to harness wisdom": from content transmission to structural training, from knowledge learning to linguistic precision (teaching students to give AI accurate instructions), from standard answers to creativity and exploratory capacity.

Those who understand "dialogue with intelligence" will not be eliminated by the times.

## Conclusion: Are You Ready?

The real question isn't whether you want to learn AI.
But rather: Are you prepared to walk alongside "non-human intelligence" while not losing your core value as a human being?

If Jensen Huang were a student today, he would learn AI.
What about you?
