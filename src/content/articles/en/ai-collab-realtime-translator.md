  After three years of Good Tape subscription fees, my usage in the final month before expiration was zero—because I already had my own tool. This article documents how someone with a background spanning theology, agricultural e-commerce, and circular economy, without an engineering identity, used AI collaboration to create a real-time multilingual meeting translation tool called "Agora Square." The tool itself isn't the point—the point is that this process made me realize: effective AI collaboration requires not the ability to code, but the ability to structure problems, break them into phases, and describe them clearly. This ability is now worth more than any programming language.
