With the rapid development of generative AI and multi-agent systems,
the market and academia are increasingly using the terms "Agent" and "Agentic."

However, the design philosophies and technical capabilities they represent are fundamentally different.
We must establish a structured taxonomy to clarify the systematic logic and application gaps between the two.

## Core Distinction: Tools vs. Partners

AI Agents and Agentic AI have clear divisions in their application scenarios:

**AI Agents**: Suited for defined tasks and automated processes. Examples include: medical decision support, industrial control, automotive navigation. They are highly reliable execution tools.

**Agentic AI**: Better equipped to handle open-ended problems. Examples include: research tasks, dynamic collaboration, autonomous planning, and linguistic interaction. They are agentic partners with self-adjustment capabilities.

## Novel Challenges of Agentic Intelligence

But the price of freedom is extremely high uncertainty.
While Agentic AI is powerful, it also faces a series of new challenges that traditional software has never encountered:

**Hallucination & brittleness**.

**Task breakdown** (failure in multi-step execution): losing direction during long-term, multi-step execution.

**Explainability & accountability**: When systems make autonomous decisions, who is responsible when errors occur?

## Conclusion: The Rise of Agentic Intelligence

Google search trends show that these two terms have risen sharply since ChatGPT's emergence.
This reflects that the market and academia are entering a turning point: **the rise of agentic intelligence**.

Future design guidance is no longer just about writing good programs.
It's about understanding how to choose appropriate architectures, finding the perfect balance between automation, perception, interaction, and self-learning.
